Eligibility<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

As a PT Solutions employee, generally you are eligible for benefits if you work at least 30 hours per week. You may enroll your eligible dependents for coverage once you are eligible.<\/p>

Your eligible dependents include your legal spouse and your children up to age 26.<\/p>

Benefits are generally effective on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment.<\/p>

You may only change coverage during annual open enrollment or within 30 days of a qualified life event.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

New Employees <\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

For new hires, benefits are effective on the first day of the month following 30 days of employment.<\/p>

You may only change coverage during annual open enrollment or within 30 days of a qualified life event.<\/p>

You may have an orientation meeting with Human Resources to review all of your benefit options, timing and deadlines.<\/p>

Evaluate Your Needs. <\/strong>Consider your prior health care usage and select plans and options that fit your lifestyle and needs.<\/p>

  • Take regular prescription medications?<\/li>
  • Anticipating surgery or non-preventive dental care?<\/li>
  • Did you experience a qualifying life event this year?<\/li>
  • Review your current plans to ensure you have the coverage you need.<\/li><\/ul>

    Review this benefits website to learn about your new and existing plan options.<\/p>

    A little bit of planning will help you select the best plans, coverage levels, and financial programs for your unique situation.<\/p>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t

    Qualified Life Event <\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
    \n\t\t\t\t
    \n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t

    Certain life events provide you a 30-day window to elect benefits or change your covered dependents.<\/p>

    What is a Qualified Life Event?<\/strong> If you experience any of the scenarios below, you may be eligible to update your benefit elections mid-year:<\/p>

    • Marriage, divorce, or legal separation (Marriage certificate\/court order)<\/div><\/li>
    • Birth or adoption of a child (Birth certificate\/hospital certification\/adoption records)<\/div><\/li>
    • Commencement or termination of adoption proceedings (Adoption records)<\/div><\/li>
    • Loss of coverage at age 26 (notice of termination from parental plan\u2019s carrier or employer with name and effective date of termination)<\/div><\/li>
    • Death of a dependent (Death Certificate)<\/div><\/li>
    • Loss or gain of other health coverage for you, your spouse, and\/or<\/div>
      dependents, including loss of parental coverage at age 26 (Confirmation or letter of loss of coverage with name and effective date\/benefit election summary)<\/div><\/li>
    • Change in employment status<\/div><\/li>
    • Change in residence due to an employment transfer for you or your spouse<\/div><\/li>
    • Change in Medicaid\/Medicare eligibility for you or a dependent (Notice from Medicaid\/Medicare)<\/div><\/li>
    • Receipt of a Qualified Medical Child Support Order (Court order)<\/div>
      \u00a0<\/div><\/li><\/ul>
      Remember, Life Event elections cannot be approved until documentation <\/strong>outlined above in parentheses is submitted.<\/strong><\/div>
      \u00a0<\/div>
      If you have questions about your eligibility or enrollment process, check with your plan administrator. You must contact Human Resources within 30 days of the event if you want to make a change to your benefit elections.
