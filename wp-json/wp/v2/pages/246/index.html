Your Health Savings Account (HSA)<\/h2>\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t
\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t
    \n \t
  • By enrolling in the High Deductible Health plan, you are qualified to make pre-tax contributions* to a Health Savings Account (HSA).<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
      \n \t
    • Your HSA funds can be used tax-free to pay for qualified medical, dental, vision and other eligible expenses, for yourself, your spouse, and your eligible tax dependents, even if they are not covered under your\nmedical plan.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
        \n \t
      • Your unused funds roll over from year to year.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
          \n \t
        • You can contribute up to an annual limit each year and watch your savings grow.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
            \n \t
          • Funds in your HSA are yours, even if you change employers or retire.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
              \n \t
            • Once your account is open, you will receive a debit card to make payments to your providers. Most in-network providers will not bill you at the time of service. You will receive a statement from the provider once the claim has been processed by the insurance company.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
                \n \t
              • Because your HSA is a tax advantaged account, you will receive two tax statements each year, detailing your contributions into and distributions from your account.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t\t\t\t\t\t<\/div>\n\t\t<\/section>\n\t\t\t\t
